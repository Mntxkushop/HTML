ABOUT computer

A computer is an electronic device that processes information, storing and retrieving it to perform calculations and tasks. It operates on a binary system, using 0s and 1s to represent data and instructions. Computers consist of hardware and software, working together to perform actions like calculations, data storage, and information management. Here's a more detailed breakdown: What it does: A computer accepts input, processes it according to instructions (programs), and produces output. Binary System: Most computers use a binary system (0s and 1s) to represent data and instructions. Hardware: This refers to the physical components of the computer, including the processor, memory, storage devices (hard drives, SSDs), and input/output devices (keyboard, mouse, monitor, printer). Software: This includes the programs and applications that instruct the hardware on how to operate, such as operating systems, web browsers, and word processors. Input: Information is fed into the computer through various devices like keyboards, mice, or sensors. Processing: The CPU (Central Processing Unit) executes instructions and performs calculations. Output: The results of processing are displayed or printed on devices like monitors or printers. Storage: Data and programs are stored in the computer's memory (RAM) and on storage devices like hard drives. Examples: Computers come in many forms, from smartphones to large servers, and are used for a wide variety of tasks.
